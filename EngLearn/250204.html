    China announces retaliatory action as Trump tariffs take effect[1]

      China has announced retaliatory retaliatory: 报复性的 adj. tariffs on some American goods, as US tariffs on all Chinese goods come into force. come into force: 生效
    此处等于 take effect / become effective

      The Chinese tariffs - due to begin on Monday - would put a 15% import tax on US coal coal: 煤 and liquefied natural gas, plus 10% on crude oil, agricultural machinery, and large-engine cars.

      The 10% tariff on all Chinese imports to the US began at 00:01 ET on Tuesday (05:01 GMT).

      China also announces an anti-monopoly investigation into Google - the company operates there, but its search engine has been blocked since 2010.

      Proposed proposed: 提议的（尚未发生） US tariffs of 25% on Canadian and Mexican goods have been postponed after last-minute talks. last-minute talks：指的是临时的、在最后一刻进行的谈判，暗示谈判是紧急和突发的，通常是在即将发生某事时才开始的。

      President Trump says the Chinese tariffs are a response to trade deficits, trade deficits: 贸易逆差
    trade surplus: 贸易盈余     and the flow of fentanyl into the US.
