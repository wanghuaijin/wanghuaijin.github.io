    1. China vice president meets with Elon Musk ahead of Trump inauguration[1]

      China's Vice President Han Zheng met on Sunday with Tesla CEO Elon Musk and other members of the U.S. business community 商界 = business circle
    business environment: 营商环境     in Washington D.C., the official Xinhua news agency reported on Monday.[2]

      Han told Musk he "welcomed Tesla and other U.S. companies to seize the opportunities and share in the benefits of China's development, and contribute to the growth of China-U.S. economic and trade relations," the report said.[3]

      U.S. President-elect Donald Trump invited Chinese President Xi Jinping to attend the inauguration after winning last year's presidential election. Xi has sent Han in his place.[4]

    2. China executes man for car rampage that killed dozens[5]

      China has executed a man found guilty 被判有罪 of killing at least 35 people in a car attack in November, in what is thought to be the deadliest attack in the country for a decade.[6]

      Fan Weiqiu, 62, injured dozens more more 表示除了前面的35人死亡，还有... when he drove his car into people exercising outside a stadium in the southern city of Zhuhai.[7]

      State media said a second man was executed for a separate attack that came days later. Xu Jiajin, 21, killed eight people in a stabbing spree 持刀砍人狂欢 at his university in the eastern city of Wuxi.[8]

      Authorities said Fan was driven by 由...推动，原因是... "dissatisfaction" over how his property 财产 had been divided following 在...之后 his divorce, while Xu carried out his attack after 因为 "failing to obtain his diploma due to poor exam results".[9]
